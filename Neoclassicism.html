Neoclassicism




Hello, art enthusiasts! Today we delve into the fascinating world of Neoclassicism, an era that revived the elegance and simplicity of classical antiquity. This movement sought beauty in harmony and proportion, drawing inspiration from ancient Greece and Rome to create works of balance and serenity. Join me as we explore how Neoclassicism made its mark on art history, inviting us to rediscover the grandeur of the past. Featuring influential artists of the time, this journey promises to unveil the timeless appeal of classical ideals.

Jacques-Louis David (1748-1825)

David was a leading figure in Neoclassicism, known for his dramatic, historically inspired compositions. His focus on classical themes and heroic subjects won him acclaim and influence.

  • El juramento de los Horacios (1784)
  • La muerte de Sócrates (1787)
  • Napoleón cruzando los Alpes (1801)
Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres (1780-1867)

Ingres was celebrated for his precise draftsmanship and adherence to classical ideals. His portraits and nudes epitomized the Neoclassical pursuit of ideal beauty and form.

  • La gran odalisca (1814)
  • El retrato de la señora Moitessier (1856)
  • El baño turco (1862)
Antonio Canova (1757-1822)

Canova was a renowned sculptor whose marble works were admired for their grace and classical inspiration. His sculptures captured the elegance of Neoclassical ideals.

  • Psique reanimada por el beso de Cupido (1787-1793)
  • Venus de Medici (1812)
  • Perseo con la cabeza de Medusa (1804-1806)
Johann Gottfried Schadow (1764-1850)

Schadow was a prominent German sculptor whose works reflected the Neoclassical focus on heroic and allegorical themes. His statues are celebrated for their refined classicism.

  • El grupo de la princesa Luise y el príncipe Heinrich (1795)
  • El monumento a Friedrich II de Prusia (1801)
  • El busto de la princesa Elisabeth (1798)
John Flaxman (1755-1826)

Flaxman was a British sculptor and illustrator known for his neoclassical reliefs and illustrations. His work was inspired by classical antiquity and is noted for its clarity and elegance.

  • Los relieves de la Odisea (1793-1794)
  • Las ilustraciones para la Divina Comedia de Dante (1824)
  • La estatua de la Paz (1814)
Benjamin West (1738-1820)

West was an American painter who contributed to Neoclassicism with his historical and allegorical paintings. His work combined classical themes with dramatic storytelling.

  • El parto de la Virgen (1775)
  • La muerte de Nelson (1806)
  • El regreso de los héroes de la guerra de independencia (1782)
